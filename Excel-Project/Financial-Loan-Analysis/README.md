# Financial Loan Analysis

## Objective
Analyze loan applications, funded amounts, repayment performance, and borrower risk characteristics to support data-driven lending and risk management decisions.

## Tools Used
- Excel (Pivot Tables, Dashboards)
- Data cleaning and transformation in Excel

## Key Analysis
- Analyzed total loan applications, funded amounts, and payments received.
- Evaluated loan performance by status (Fully Paid, Charged Off, Current).
- Assessed borrower risk using interest rate and debt-to-income (DTI).
- Analyzed loan distribution by purpose, term length, employment length, and home ownership.
- Reviewed geographic distribution of loan applications across states.

## Key Insights
- Over **86% of loans are good loans**, while bad loans account for approximately **14%**, indicating relatively strong portfolio quality.
- Total funded amount exceeds **$435M**, with payments received of over **$473M**, reflecting solid repayment performance.
- Charged-off loans account for a significantly smaller share of the total funded amount compared to fully paid loans.
- Loans with **36-month terms** dominate the portfolio compared to 60-month loans.
- Borrowers with longer employment histories account for a higher share of loan applications.
- Certain loan purposes (debt consolidation and credit cards) represent the largest share of applications.
- Interest rates and DTI are higher for charged-off loans, highlighting risk concentration.
